Who Was She?

Lucille Tenazas is a Filipino-born graphic designer, educator, and design leader whose career spans more than four decades. Born in Manila in 1953, she is known for her experimental, postmodern approach to typography and visual communication, where language and meaning play a central role. In 1982, she founded Tenazas Design, working with major cultural and educational institutions including SFMOMA, Stanford University, and Chronicle Books. Alongside her professional practice, Tenazas has had a lasting impact on design education as the founding chair of the MFA Design program at California College of the Arts and later as the Henry Wolf Professor of Communication Design at Parsons School of Design. A former national president of AIGA and recipient of the 2013 AIGA Medal, Tenazas remains an influential figure whose work bridges conceptual thinking, education, and contemporary design practice.
